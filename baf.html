An ecosystem
for the world

British Alpaca Fashion began with a commitment to the welfare of our people, our animals, and our planet. From our design studio at Weekfield Farm in Somerset, we steward a growing portfolio of luxury alpaca brands — each distinct in character, united in values.

We view fashion not as a product but as an ecosystem — one that connects the regenerative pastures of Exmoor to artisans across the world, from Somerset to the hills of Biella to the highlands of Asia.

Our Vision

To combine creativity with nature and animal welfare — proving that the future of fashion is circular.

Hall of
Fame

Eighteen years of growing an ecosystem — from a single farm in Somerset to brands that span Exmoor, Biella, and the East. Every year, a step further into the circle.

2008 The Beginning David receives Champagne & Truffles as a birthday gift. British Alpaca Fashion established at Weekfield Farm, Somerset.
2011 R&D & First Runway Research into 100% British alpaca yarns at heritage mills. First Fashion Show at Phyllis Court Club, Henley-on-Thames.
2012 National Recognition Winners of the Conservative Nationwide Start Up Hub Competition, UK.
2013 No. 10 Downing Street Exhibitions at Textile Forum London. Invited to meet PM David Cameron at Downing Street.
2015 Savile Row & Peru D&A Super 120s purchased by Richard Anderson at Savile Row. Invited to Arequipa Fiesta in Peru.
2016 Shima Seiki Expansion Knitwear production expanded using Japanese Shima Seiki machines — heritage craft meets precision technology.
2018 Silver Screen EkoAlpaka yarns selected by John Smedley. Textiles supplied to Murder on the Orient Express.
2019 Future Fashion Factory Smart clothing with conductive wires showcased at the Future Fashion Factory Exhibition.
2022 EU Fashion for Change EU Fashion for Change Programme. Digital product passport introduced for full supply-chain transparency.
2025 World & Peace Selected for Ambiente Frankfurt. Inaugural peace exhibition in Hiroshima, Japan.

The Fibre

Nature's finest
luxury fibre

Alpaca fibre is eight times warmer than wool, hypoallergenic, and completely free of lanolin. Ours comes from animals that live freely on the regenerative pastures of Exmoor — never stressed, never over-shorn.
